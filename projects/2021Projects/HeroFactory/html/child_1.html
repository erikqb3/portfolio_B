Life and Family: the Greatest Story and Most Important Work of Art

Hi! My name is Kire. Though I am an animator by trade, I also have experience with creative writing, illustration, music composing and performing, and web design. Referring back to animating, I got my degree in Animations from BYU. I've worked in several animation industries that focused on 2D cartoon animations for children's programs.

Note: Kire L. Elrid is my pen name and I currently have not graduated yet nor worked in any animation industry of any sort. I am not intentionally fabricating my identity, I simply am chronologically impaired.

Tales of Eight

It has been over fifteen years since the rebel forces and the government of [name] started the 7th Season of War. Those on neither side are tired of the bloodshed and unrest that has become a normality. It is up to eight unexpected characters to put an end to it all, but such a worthy cause hasn't crossed the minds of any of them. Regardless of the fact that each of them has a part to play in the war, they each have their own desires to fulfill, their own life to live, their own tale to tell.

Tales of Eight is a collection of introductory short stories of characters in the trilogy Fruits of Rebellion.The story starts from the perspective of RVon Varundae (reptillian projectile user)and proceeds to the other characters, ending with Florence (bounty hunting botanist); who carries the mantle of "main character" throughout the rest of the main trilogy. "The stories were originally starting projects to get my creativity flowing at the start of my journey as a story teller, but after deeper consideration, I fell in love with the characters I created and had to help them progress to greater heights in their own world." (Kire L. Elrid)

Emag City

In a city where the main source of economy is games and entertainment, young Kobe Shoryu dreams of making his way up to the top! Sadly though, his skills aren't anywhere close to even "Rookie" material. In order to reclaim a lost chance of making a name for himself, Kobe's got to find himself a trainer; and not just any trainer; a one that was once a Legend.

However, when fate leads Kobe to run into Lloyd Shatler, the Legend is less than enthusiastic about being Kobe's trainer. Despite the obvious loathing Lloyd has towards videogames, Kobe sees the skills Lloyd possesses that won him the title of a Legend and is determined to make Lloyd his trainer. "The story itself has a bit of my own past experiences woven into it, with a few of the characters being tributes to people I know" (Kire L. Elrid)

Gimmick Wars

What does one do when given remarkable power that can change the world? That is a question Blake Giftfield has to figure out and figure out fast. With the uprising of gimmick users both good and evil, Blake needs to figure out where he stands in order to prevent global anarchy. Does he have what it takes though to harness his own gimmick while also avoiding detection?

"This story all started out with my fascination with super heroes and their abilities! It was so cool seeing interesting characters with interesting quirks that I wanted to see if I could make my own. Coming up with powers was the easy part, but coming up with interesting characters that matched them was trickier. It was easy to get caught up more on the "world" aspect behind it all and ignore the "story" and even the "character" aspect when day dreaming about gimmicks of my own, but in the end; it's people and their stories that are more interesting" (Kire L. Elrid)

Reign of Slyme

The year is sometime in the distant future on a planet much more hostile than earth. Humans on that planet live below ground to avoid the monstrous titans from above; however, that doesn't mean there aren't dangers belong.

After a skirmish with some enemy kingdoms, Seth Cummings loses his life; yet somehow he returns just in time for his wedding with the princess of The Central Gorge. But before the vows are taken; an attack is launched by the enemies, a horrific truth is revealed, and the slymes have made their entrances.

"The concept seemed a bit dark to me at first, but after much prayer and studying I was able to shed some light in the story!" (Kire L. Elrid)

Trapped in a Sword

Legend tells a sword of immortality. The wielder of the blade is granted infinite power and the strength of a thousand men. But such a prize comes at a high cost. Those who are in possession of it don't stay that way long; for the only a person can have access to the swords power is by killing the owner with it. The chain of owners and victims stretches on for millennium.

Hoping bring an end to the attacks on his master's life, Yamada Kiroshi runs away with the blade and seeks to bring an end to the curse the blade brings to the world. Little does he know that not only would success save his master's life, but the life of another as well. Someone very close to the blade and has lived with through the deaths of thousands throughout her existence, and she's had enough of it all

"The world is in need of light, not just the world as a whole, but the worlds of individuals as well." (Kire L. Elrid)
