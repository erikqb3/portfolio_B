Overview

Purpose

Hero Factory is a website where artists who strive to create wholesome, uplifting, and awesome art can connect with other such artists. Under the creation of Kire L. Elrid, Hero Factory is similar to that of a job board; people can post project ideas they want to carry out and those looking for work and experience can offer their service. The website will have links that send users to various pages where they can learn more about the creator of the website and his work, connect with others, provide and introduction of their selves, get recommendations to different motivational and helpful resources, and day-dream their starting project ideas.

Audience

My audience will consist of artists that strive to create wholesome, uplifting, and awesome art and need help in their projects. A vast majority of them will be English speakers ranging from young middle schoolers to college students and beyond. Most of them would access the web page in search of either a proposed project they can contribute their talents to, other artists to recruit for the making of their own projects, or simply inspiration and a better understanding in order to improve their work. Most important of all is that they are people who share similar values and morals pertaining to wholesome art and want to connect with others to support them in their standards.

Content

Why will people come to my site?People will come to my site because they are in search of two things. One is that they are looking for projects of others that they can offer their service and talents to in order to develop their own artistic skills and gain experience working with others. The other thing they are looking for are fellow artists that share the same standards and values as they do in terms of virtuous art. There is an increasing amount of inappropriate content being fed into the entertainment and media industry; which gives reason for those who want to provide the world with better to conjoin together and support each other in their united and personal endeavors to create good art.

What questions will your visitors expect your site to answer for them? Visitors will come to my website with questions such as “who can help me in my project”, “who is providing a project idea that I can offer my talents to in order to gain experience and connections”, and “who else is there that shares similar standards in terms of wholesome entertainment and the importance of?” In essence, the main purpose of this website is to help others find out who’s out there.
Other questions that visitors might have would involve what’s out there. “What can help me get out of my artistic rut?” “What is something others are doing that I can learn from and apply?” “What’s a good idea I can come up with or build off of?”

What types of activities will visitors want to complete on your site? My website will involve the activity of interacting with others. People can pitch out project ideas and ask for individuals with specific experiences and skill sets. They can also offer suggestions of what has helped them in their artistic journey and their comments on other’s suggestions. They can also provide introductory information about themselves, samples of their own work, and contact information so that they can assist those that are searching.
Along with the activity of interacting is the activity of learning. Visitors can learn from the work of others (via Contributors’ Introductions) and discover what works for them and what they can apply into their own work.

What information is necessary to complete any actions your site offers? In order to be a full contributor of the website, a person needs to register themselves which involves creating an introduction post. The introduction post will ask questions such as “What kind of art do you specialize in?”, “How long have you been working with this art medium?”, “Are you self-taught or do you have professional training? (ie college)”, “Are you a member of the church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints?”. and other questions that provide viewers to figure out how good you are and if you are someone they would want to work with. Those making an introduction post must also post samples of their work (whether it be a word/PDF document, a picture, audio, video, etc. They can also choose whether or not to add links to their own personal social media pages; but despite whether or not they want to, they must provide some samples of their work to show to others that they are legit.

Why will your visitors come to your site instead of another to get this information or complete the actions? Visitors will come to my site instead of another’s because along with providing invitations from others to join in on projects, my website focuses on artists who stand firm in their standards of wholesome and uplifting art. Those who do not promote pornography, vulgar language, disturbing imagery, controversy arguments, and other displeasing aspects that pollutes the entertainment and media people (especially youth) consume.

Project Proposals

Layed out in the format of a discussion board, participants can give a brief summary of an idea they have for a project and list off the kind of experienced individuals they need in order to make their idea a reality. If you're an aspiring artist who wants a taste of what it's like collaborating with others, you can browse the chat to find a project that satisfies your fancy. Whether it be you're an animator planning on making a YouTube video and looking for a voice actor, a writer hoping to publish a novel and looking for an editor and an illustrator, a composer simply trying to beef up his portfolio and looking for a handful of musicians, or anything in between; post your intentions and invitations for others to come help you out!

Motivational Resources

Looking for resources that can help you increase your artistic confidence and hone your skills? Click on the "Motivaional Resources" button and find a descripted list of online and physical materials. Such materials involve suggested YouTubers, insightful books, [adj] online courses, and [adj] software. None of the listed resouces can actually be purchased or aquired from this website, but the list and descriptions serves as recommendations. Viewers can provide their comments concerning the recommended resources and even provide recommendations of their own.

Day-Dream Productions!

Have you ever wanted to create an enthralling work of art, but never knew where to begin or lacked confidence to start? Day Dream Productions (DDP) is a website that helps users visualize the piece of art they want to create! To start, DDP supplies a variety of prompts and questions to spark imagination. The website then displays the user’s responses in an organized outline. Finally, the result of the exercise is saved onto their account for further contemplation.
While this website is still under construction, DDP is intdended to motivate anyone to create. You don't have to have the skills or the experiences (not yet at least). All you need right now is permission from yourself to dream something, plan something, and create something.
